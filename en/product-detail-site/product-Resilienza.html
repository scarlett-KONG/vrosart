This work depicts a feminine figure rising from the waves,

a land once free, powerful, and nourished by the richness of its natural elements.

Crowned in gold and draped in a white robe blending seamlessly with the sea, the young woman

symbol of the island’s indomitable and vital force.

Her upward gaze expresses dignity and the hope for a brighter future, despite the wounds of the past.

Around her, two human hands attempt to despoil her: one tearing away her spear —

a symbol of protection and autonomy — while the other tries to unravel her ocean-like robe,

a metaphor for the gradual plundering of Sicilian wealth throughout time.

The red fish, peaceful witnesses to the scene, evoke the life and fragile beauty that persist beneath the surface.

Thus, amid turmoil and loss, the woman-Sicily stands tall — sovereign, resilient, and unbroken.
